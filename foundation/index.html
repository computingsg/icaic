About

The ICAIC Foundation supports the Inter-Collegiate Artificial Intelligence Contest (ICAIC), bringing together students, universities, and partners to develop practical AI skills and scientific thinking.

We aim to create an accessible pathway for students to learn and demonstrate competence across modern AI topics: machine learning, reasoning, data-centric development, evaluation, safety, and deployment.

The contest

What it tests

Building and evaluating AI systems under constraints: data, compute, time, and real-world metrics.

How it runs

Multi-stage structure: local qualifiers, regional rounds, and a world final. Formats may include offline evaluation, live challenges, and explainability review.

What it rewards

Rigor, reproducibility, creativity, teamwork, and responsible AI practice. Success is more than a leaderboard score.

Mission and vision

Mission

To develop the next generation of AI practitioners and researchers by running a premier collegiate AI contest and supporting year-round learning, mentorship, and community.

Vision

A world where students everywhere can learn AI deeply, build systems responsibly, and collaborate across borders to solve meaningful problems.

Principles

  • Accessibility: lower barriers for schools and students.
  • Scientific rigor: clear baselines, evaluation, and reproducibility.
  • Responsibility: safety, fairness, and transparency by design.

Programs

Educational resources

Curated learning paths, starter kits, open datasets, and evaluation harnesses to help teams get ready.

Regional support for universities to host qualifiers, run workshops, and build local communities.

Industry partnerships

Sponsors and partners can contribute compute, mentorship, and problem themes aligned with responsible impact.
